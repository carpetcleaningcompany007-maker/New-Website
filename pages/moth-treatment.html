Moth activity in carpets and rugs is a specialist issue and needs to be taken seriously, especially where wool and other natural fibres are involved. The visible damage people notice is often only part of the problem. By the time thinning patches or irregular loss of pile are obvious, larvae may have been active for some time in quieter parts of the room, around edges, under furniture or in lower disturbance areas. Our certified moth treatment service is aimed at carpets, rugs and suitable textile furnishings where professional advice and treatment are needed. Customers usually contact us after noticing bald patches, loose fibres, unusual wear that does not fit normal traffic patterns, or small insects in rooms with wool carpets or rugs. We inspect the circumstances, discuss the likely pattern of damage and explain the role of treatment, cleaning and housekeeping measures in tackling the problem. Searches such as carpet moth treatment, moth treatment for wool carpet, rug moth treatment and textile pest treatment are highly specific and often urgent. They are also areas where generic cleaning advice is not enough.

Frequently asked questions

What are signs of carpet moth activity?
Thinning patches, irregular fibre loss, damage around edges or under furniture and unusual wear in wool carpets or rugs can all be warning signs.

Do you provide certified moth treatment?
Yes. We provide certified treatment for suitable textile pest problems as part of a professional fibre care service.

Can moth damaged carpet be cleaned as well?
Cleaning may be possible alongside treatment where appropriate, but the exact approach depends on the condition and extent of the damage.
